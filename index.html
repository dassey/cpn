Command Post Node (CPN) Setup Guide

Comprehensive Manual for BnCPN Installation & Configuration

Mission Overview

What is a Command Post Node (CPN)?

The Command Post Node (CPN) is a tactical communications system designed to provide secure, high-capacity voice and data communications for battalion-level headquarters and smaller units. It serves as the primary communications backbone for field operations, enabling connectivity to higher echelon networks including WIN-T (Warfighter Information Network-Tactical) and the Global Information Grid (GIG).

Key Capabilities:

  • Secure voice and data communications
  • Internet Protocol (IP) routing and switching
  • Satellite communications (SATCOM) connectivity
  • Integration with higher echelon networks
  • Support for multiple security classifications
  • Mobile and deployable operations

Quick Start Tip

Review all safety procedures and ensure all required personnel are available before beginning setup. Having experienced signal personnel is critical to mission success.

Manual Structure

This guide is organized into sequential steps. Complete each step's checklists before proceeding to the next to ensure a successful and safe setup.

Prerequisites and Safety Procedures

Critical safety requirements and pre-deployment checks

CRITICAL SAFETY WARNING

  • • **High voltage equipment** - ensure proper grounding before operation.
  • • **Radio frequency (RF) radiation hazard** - maintain safe distances during transmission.
  • • **Heavy equipment lifting** - use proper lifting techniques and equipment.
  • • **Classified material handling** - follow all COMSEC procedures.

Required Personnel

Primary Team

  • • Signal Officer (25A) - 1 required
  • • Information Technology Specialist (25B) - 2 required
  • • Satellite Communications Operator (25S) - 1 required

Support Team

  • • Power Generation Equipment Repairer (91D) - 1 required
  • • Motor Transport Operator (88M) - 2 required
  • • Security Personnel - As required by mission

Security Clearance Requirements

  • Secret Clearance: All personnel handling classified equipment.
  • Top Secret/SCI: Personnel configuring SIPR networks and classified routing.
  • COMSEC Custodian: Personnel handling cryptographic materials.

Pre-Deployment Checklist

  • Conduct site survey and security assessment.
  • Verify all personnel security clearances and training.
  • Complete equipment inventory and operational checks.
  • Obtain COMSEC materials and cryptographic keys.
  • Coordinate transportation and logistics support.
  • Review and approve frequency allocations and call signs.

Cisco Routers & Switches

Equipment Inspection Checklist

  • Verify all equipment serial numbers against property book.
  • Conduct power-on self-test (POST) for all electronic equipment.

  • Verify antenna systems are complete and undamaged.
  • Test generator operation and fuel system integrity.
  • Confirm availability of technical manuals and documentation.

Site Preparation and Physical Setup

Site survey, preparation, and shelter deployment

Safety Notice: Shelter deployment requires coordination of multiple personnel and heavy equipment. Ensure all safety procedures are followed.

Site Selection Requirements

Location Requirements

  • Size: Minimum 100m x 100m cleared area.
  • Elevation: High ground with clear line of sight to the southern sky (for CONUS).
  • Access: Vehicle accessible for heavy equipment.
  • Security: Defensible position with multiple egress routes.

Environmental Considerations

  • RF Environment: Survey for interference sources (power lines, other transmitters).
  • Weather: Assess prevailing wind patterns and ensure good drainage.
  • Ground: Must be firm and as level as possible.
  • Vegetation: Clear any obstacles for antenna positioning.

Shelter and Antenna Setup

Shelter Deployment

  • Position shelter using GPS coordinates from plan.
  • Orient door away from prevailing winds.
  • Deploy stabilizer jacks and level the shelter.
  • Connect grounding system to the site earth ground.

Antenna Installation

  • Assemble antenna reflector and feed assembly.
  • Mount antenna and connect RF cables.
  • Perform initial pointing using GPS and compass.
  • Ensure proper separation from other antennas to minimize interference.

Site Setup Verification Checklist

  • Complete site survey and environmental assessment.
  • Deploy and secure ANTTC-64D(V)3 shelter system.
  • Install and test environmental control systems (ECU).
  • Deploy and align all antenna systems.
  • Establish perimeter security and access control.
  • Verify power generation and distribution systems are ready.
4

Power Systems Installation and Testing

Generator setup, power distribution, and grounding

High Voltage Warning: Only qualified personnel (91D) should perform electrical installations. Follow all safety lockout/tagout procedures.

Generator & UPS Setup

MEP-831A Generator Setup

  • Position generator minimum 10 feet from structures.
  • Connect fuel system and prime if necessary.
  • Perform pre-start inspection (oil, coolant, battery).
  • Start generator and verify stable voltage (120V/208V) and frequency (60Hz).

UPS Configuration

  • Connect critical loads (e.g., routers, servers, crypto) to the UPS.
  • Ensure UPS is connected to a dedicated circuit from the distribution panel.
  • Test battery runtime by simulating a power failure.
  • Configure automatic shutdown scripts for servers if applicable.

Grounding and Bonding

Earth Grounding System

  • Install a minimum of 3 ground rods, 8 feet deep, spaced 6 feet apart.
  • Connect rods with #4 AWG bare copper wire.
  • Test ground resistance (must be **≤10 ohms**).

Equipment Bonding

  • Bond all equipment chassis, racks, and the shelter frame to the grounding system.
  • Install surge protection devices on power and data lines.
  • Verify electrical continuity of all bonds.

Power System Verification Checklist

  • Install and test MEP-831A generator under load.
  • Configure power distribution panel and label all circuits.
  • Install and test UPS systems for critical loads.
  • Install grounding system and verify resistance is ≤10 ohms.
  • Test automatic transfer switches and emergency shutdown.
  • Verify all safety systems and protective devices (GFCIs).
5

Network Configuration and Integration

IP configuration, routing setup, and network security

Network Design: The CPN implements a multi-tier architecture supporting both classified (SIPR) and unclassified (NIPR) traffic through separate, physically or logically isolated security domains.

Core Network Setup

Switch & VLAN Configuration

  • Load baseline configuration on switches.
  • Configure VLANs to segment traffic (e.g., VLAN 100 for SIPR, VLAN 200 for NIPR).
  • Implement port security to restrict unauthorized device access.
  • Configure trunk ports to carry tagged VLAN traffic between switches and routers.

Router & Routing Protocols

  • Configure router interfaces for each VLAN.
  • Set up **OSPF** for internal routing within the CPN.
  • Configure **BGP** for external routing to the WIN-T network.
  • Implement Access Control Lists (ACLs) to filter traffic between networks.

TACLANE and Security Configuration

TACLANE Integration

  • Physically install TACLANE encryptors between the router and the "black" (unsecured) side of the satellite link.
  • Load cryptographic keys using a KIV-7M or other fill device.
  • Configure the TACLANE with the correct IP settings to create a secure tunnel.
  • Verify traffic is being successfully encrypted and decrypted.

Network Configuration Checklist

  • Configure WIN-T POP and core switches with baseline settings.
  • Set up VLAN segmentation for all security domains.
  • Configure OSPF and BGP routing protocols.
  • Implement security policies, ACLs, and firewall rules.
  • Install and configure TACLANE devices with current keys.
  • Test network connectivity and routing tables within the CPN.
6

Satellite Communications Setup

Antenna pointing, modem configuration, and link establishment

RF Safety Warning: Maintain minimum safe distances from transmitting antennas. Coordinate with RF safety officer before energizing systems.

Antenna Pointing and Modem Setup

Antenna Pointing & Alignment

  • Use the satellite acquisition plan to determine correct Azimuth, Elevation, and Polarization.
  • Use a spectrum analyzer to find the satellite beacon.
  • Perform fine-tuning adjustments to peak the signal for maximum strength.
  • Lock the antenna position.

Satellite Modem Configuration

  • Configure the modem with the correct transmit/receive frequencies, data rates, and modulation type per the mission plan.
  • Set the modem's IP addresses to integrate with the router.
  • Enable automatic power control (APC) to maintain link stability.

Link Establishment and Monitoring

Acquiring the Link

  • Coordinate with the satellite network control station to bring up the link.
  • Enable the modem transmitter and verify power levels.
  • Monitor key performance indicators (KPIs) like **Eb/No** (signal quality) and **BER** (bit error rate).
  • The target Eb/No is typically >8.0 dB, with a BER of <1x10⁻⁶.

SATCOM Configuration Checklist

  • Install and align satellite antenna system.
  • Configure satellite modem with correct RF and network parameters.
  • Establish satellite link and verify performance meets mission standards.
  • Integrate SATCOM with network routing via the default route.
  • Configure QoS and traffic management for the satellite link.
  • Test end-to-end connectivity to a higher echelon node.
7

System Testing and Validation

Comprehensive testing procedures and acceptance criteria

Test Objective: Verify that all CPN systems meet operational requirements and can support mission-critical communications before declaring Full Operational Capability (FOC).

Network and Communications Testing

Network Performance Tests

  • Conduct `ping` and `traceroute` tests to all key nodes.
  • Verify DNS resolution and DHCP lease assignment.
  • Run `iperf` or similar tools to test throughput between LAN clients and across the WAN link.
  • Measure latency and jitter for real-time applications.

Application & Voice Tests

  • Make test calls using secure voice (STE) and VoIP phones.
  • Initiate a video teleconference (VTC) session if equipped.
  • Transfer data files of various sizes to test FTP/SMB services.
  • Send and receive test emails through the system.

Security Validation

  • Verify firewall ACLs are correctly blocking and permitting traffic.
  • Confirm VPN tunnels are active and passing encrypted traffic.
  • Attempt to access restricted resources to ensure access controls are working.
  • Check logs for any security violations or anomalies.

Final System Acceptance Checklist

  • Power systems and ECU operate correctly under full load.
  • End-to-end connectivity is established on NIPR and SIPR networks.
  • Secure and non-secure voice and data services are fully functional.
  • All security controls and encryption devices are active and correctly configured.
  • Satellite link is stable and meets performance requirements.
  • All tests are documented in the system logbook.

System Handover and Documentation

Finalizing documentation and completing the mission

Final Documentation and Training

Final Documentation

  • Save final router and switch configurations.
  • Update the network diagrams to reflect the as-built state.
  • Complete all entries in the equipment and site logbooks.
  • Archive all test results for future reference.

Operator Training

  • Brief operators on system startup and shutdown procedures.
  • Review basic troubleshooting steps for common issues.
  • Confirm COMSEC handling and key management procedures.
  • Provide contact information for Tier 2/3 support.

Mission Completion

CPN is Fully Mission Capable

The Command Post Node has passed all tests and is ready for handover. All systems are operational and providing secure, reliable communications.
